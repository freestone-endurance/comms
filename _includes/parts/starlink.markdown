## Data - Starlink

The Starlink is a SpaceX satellite data system that gives us a reliable, high-bandwidth connection anywhere with a clear view of the sky. We use these to establish excellent connections from anywhere on the course.

### Which Starlink Do I Have?

We use three different models, and you may receive any of them depending on what's available. The idea is the same for all three — give the dish a clear view of the sky, connect the cables, and wait 1–2 minutes for it to come online — but the hardware differs:

- **Starlink Mini** — the smallest, all-in-one unit. The WiFi router is built into the dish, so it's just the dish plus a power cable. Fastest to set up.
- **Gen 2** (rectangular dish) — has a separate rectangular WiFi router. The dish plugs into the router, and the router plugs into power. Its cables are the most delicate of the three, so be gentle.
- **Gen 3** (standard dish with a built-in kickstand) — has a separate WiFi router. Same idea as Gen 2: dish → router → power.

If you're not sure which one you have, that's OK — the setup steps below work for all of them.

### Starlink - Setup

- Find a good spot for the dish with a clear view of the sky and no trees in a 20ft x 20ft area near your AS.
- If you have a generator, plug in via a long extension cord so the generator sits away from your AS.
- If you have a battery with solar panel(s), plug into the battery and set up the solar panel(s) (daisy chain them if more than one).
- Connect the cables: dish → router → power. **Push every connector all the way in.** A cable that isn't fully seated is the #1 cause of a Starlink that won't come online (especially on Gen 2 and Gen 3).
- Give it 1–2 minutes to boot, then connect your device to the WiFi (see Credentials).
- You'll have internet quickly, but it will keep improving over the next 5–20 minutes as the dish aligns itself.
- Verify you're online by running a "Refresh Data" in OST Remote.
- Let CommsHQ know you're online by texting them (via Discord).

### What To Do If... (Starlink)

- **It won't come online:** Unplug and firmly reconnect every cable, then power-cycle it and give it another 1–2 minutes. A connector that isn't fully seated is by far the most common problem.
- **It was working, then quit:** Starlinks can overheat and shut themselves down. Unplug it, move it into the shade, and let it cool for at least 30 minutes. Track runners on paper and relay times to CommsHQ in the meantime.
- **Protect the cables:** They're more delicate than they look (Gen 2 especially). Avoid sharp kinks, twisting, and foot or vehicle traffic.
- **Rain:** Never let any equipment sit in standing water. The dishes are weather-resistant, but water pooling on the dish face degrades performance — a plastic bin makes a good cover as long as water doesn't pool on top. The router is less weather-resistant than the dish, so keep it protected.
- **Still stuck:** Keep tracking on paper, relay times to CommsHQ, and let CommsHQ know you're having equipment trouble.
